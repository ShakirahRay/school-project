The Upper and Lower Quads are located on the east side of the Academic Quad to either side of the Fitness Center. The Quads feature single and double rooms, as well as a limited number of special use apartments. The Quads are home to the First-Year Residential Experience (FYRE), Honors housing, Substance Free, and the HOME Residential Learning Community.

The Upper Quads (closest to the Towers) include: Boothroyd, Hilliard, Holmes, Hood, Rowland and Tallcott Halls. The Lower Quads include: Bogart, Clarke, Eastman (Area Office), Landon and Lyon Halls.

Each Quad Hall is complete with a TV and study lounge, a kitchen, vending area, laundry facilities, shared (single-gender) bathrooms and stairwell balconies. There are no elevators in the Quads, but certain buildings provide for ADA access.

The Towers are located on the south side of the Academic Quad and are the most visible landmarks on the Ithaca College campus. The Towers are a part of the First-Year Residential Experience. They feature single and double rooms, and are ideal for students who desire a traditional residence hall experience.

Each Tower is complete with two elevators a TV lounge, a kitchen, vending area, and laundry facilities. There is also a study lounge on each floor, and residents' mailboxes with a staffed mailroom located in each lobby.

Floors in the Towers are designated to a single gender with a shared single-gender bathroom on each. Each floor houses between 13 to 27 residents including a Resident Assistant.

In addition, the Towers Dining Hall and the Sub Connection are connected to each Tower by an indoor walkway.

Finding Internships

Deciding on, finding, and applying for jobs or internships is an important process. Career Services offers individualized career counseling, job/internship search assistance, various online and print resources, and multiple on- and off-campus events to help you along the way.

Preparing for an Interview

Interview preparation is a critical component of a job or internship search. Preparing for the interview is almost as important as the interview itself. The key to successful interviewing is knowing who you are and knowing the organization with which you are interviewing.

Take a look at these handy tips to get a sense of how you can prepare!

  • Research, research, research. Before going to an interview, you will want to learn more about the company, department and industry trends. Depending on the company, you can find this information on their website, through the news or by word of mouth.
  • Schedule a mock interview with one of our professional staff members. Mock interviews are intended to help you sharpen your interviewing skills and are useful when you are applying to graduate school, for an internship or a full-time employment opportunity. Mock interviews are generally conducted in person, though if you are unable to visit our office, we can do a mock phone interview with you.
  • Dress for success! First impressions are extremely important to employers. Make sure you dress appropriately for the industry in which you want to work or intern. Practice wearing your interviewing clothes so that you are comfortable in them.
  • Practice your hand shake. It is important that it be firm. Don't give someone a "dead fish" hand shake. Conversely, don't break someone's bones.
  • Practice making eye contact when you're talking with someone. And don't forget to smile!
